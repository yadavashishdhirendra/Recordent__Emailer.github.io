Hello,

Delayed payments is a mammoth and a deep-rooted problem plaguing most businesses in India- whether MSMEs or Large corporates! We, at Recordent are looking to solve this problem with a unique approach - combining data aggregation and technology, supplemental to the efforts taken by GOI to ease the cash-flow issues.

Through a credit-bureau like system, we capture the payment history of businesses to other businesses while helping them collect their outstanding dues on time. Our platform allows businesses to check the payment profile of other businesses or their credit report so that they can customize their terms of engagement with their buyers.

THE PROBLEM AT HAND

  • Lack of data transparency on payment history impacts businesses in making informed decisions on new customer risks
  • Lack of consequences coupled with slow and expensive legal processes makes collections difficult
  • Lack of awareness on the long-term benefits of maintaining good credit score and supplier-relationships

HOW DOES IT WORK?

The way Credit Bureaus use a scoring mechanism to ensure payments to Lenders are paid on time, we are creating a Bureau for businesses to pay other businesses on time.

  • We send automated notifications to your customers to pay "you" on time so that "they" can maintain a positive payment track record
  • We notify your customer that other businesses will be able to view "their" payment history, so please pay "you" on time
  • Payment link provided with each notification to nudge customers to pay, ads a new payment option for you

This approach has worked wonders for many of our clients - including some who have collected over 48% of their receivables ranging from 6 months to 2 year over-dues!

FEATHERS IN OUR CAP

  • Strategic partnership with Equifax allows Recordent Members to access bureau credit reports. Records available of over 25mn businesses and 550mn individuals.
  • Trade Credit Insurance feature allows Recordent Members to insure their receivables against defaults/payment delays (through tie-ups with Insurance players).
  • A Positive Network Effect means more companies from the same industry improve the chances of success for all the companies. The case of customers shifting from one company to another, after defaulting with one company is a very common phenomenon.
